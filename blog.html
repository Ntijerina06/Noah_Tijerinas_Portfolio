Why The Laugh?

Sorry for the delay in posting. My life has been pretty stagnant recently. Nothing too crazy. Some news is that I got my first software engineering internship, and I will be a Python Dev at Duarte. I am super excited to contribute to their new product coming out. I also have been applying to a lot of Stanford summer research programs. The one I really want to get into is SURF, but when looking at the pictures, it looks like none of the accepted applicants come from a community college, but I am hopeful. A realization that I have made is people think I am crazy. Whenever I tell someone about my future aspirations, they just laugh at me. I tell them I want to get into a top 15 university, have a successful start-up, or go into quant. They chuckle. As a community college student, I feel people look down on me and think I am not competent enough because I didn't get into a good college out of high school. I hope it is a good thing that they are laughing because one day, I know I will accomplish great things. Had A Taste

The only downside is how little spare time I have on some days. Since I work, it can be stressful and tiring. This Wednesday and Tuesday, I had a little taste of the grind; Tuesday, I was at school all day, then I rushed to cross country practice, ran 7 miles, then I had to join my Stanford SSG meeting, then do homework for a few hours, lastly sleep. On Wednesday, I had school at 10:30 am, which was nice because I got to go on a walk with my mom. Still, I woke up early to do homework. I went to class, went to cross country, went home, ate food, and went to work; then, at around 9 pm, I came home, did homework, and worked on a club constitution. I'm nervous about what will happen when I have clubs, but I am hopeful for what will come. Also, I had a PR at 26:16 minutes for 4 miles at my cross-country meet on Friday. Thanks, see you eventually :).

September 23, 2024

Had the Itch

I was waiting for the end of this week to make a post on my blog, but I have been very excited to write. I want to reveal more about my personality. I am currently 18 years old, and I am going to De Anza Community College. I love to skateboard. I have been doing it my whole life but got into it during COVID. I have a sponsor, and I participate in local competitions. I also get these itchs to play chess, and I am currently creating lots of coding projects, which led to this blog. In high school, I didn't know how the college system worked. I thought that if I had a decent GPA and some volunteering experience, I would have a 10% chance of getting into UC Berkeley. I took four APs, two dual enrollment, 120+ hours of volunteering, and two clubs, and I had a 3.5 GPA. As a computer science major, I applied to 12 schools, including the University of Oregon - Harvey Mudd and UCLA. I got into the University of Oregon and UCSC off the waitlist. That is why I go to Community College. See you eventually. :)
